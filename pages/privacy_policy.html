1. Overview

This Privacy Policy explains how we process personal data when you visit this website ("Online Offering"). We only process data that is technically necessary to provide and secure the website, and data you send to us when contacting us.

2. Legal bases

If you are in the EEA/UK, we process personal data only if a legal basis applies. The most relevant bases for this website are:

  • Legitimate interests (Art. 6(1)(f) GDPR) – operating and securing the website
  • Contract / pre-contractual steps (Art. 6(1)(b) GDPR) – if your request relates to a service/contract
  • Consent (Art. 6(1)(a) GDPR) – only if we explicitly ask for it (e.g., future non-essential cookies)

3. Hosting & server log files

When you access this website, the hosting provider processes technical data to deliver the content and ensure security. This may include IP address, date/time of access, requested page/file, referrer URL, browser/OS information, and status codes.

  • Purpose: delivery of content, stability, troubleshooting, abuse prevention
  • Legal basis: legitimate interests (Art. 6(1)(f) GDPR)
  • Retention: typically [7–30] days (depending on host), then deletion or anonymization

4. Cookies & local storage

We do not use cookies for advertising or tracking. The website may use strictly necessary browser storage (e.g., to remember basic UI settings). This storage remains on your device and can be deleted in your browser settings.

If we introduce non-essential cookies in the future, we will request your consent beforehand.

5. Contact

If you contact us (e.g., by email), we process the information you provide (such as your email address and message content) to respond to your request.

  • Legal basis: Art. 6(1)(b) GDPR (contract-related) or Art. 6(1)(f) GDPR (general communication)
  • Retention: until the request is completed, plus a reasonable period for follow-up; longer if required by law

6. Your rights

If you are located in the EEA/UK, you may have the following rights (depending on the applicable law):

  • Right of access (Art. 15 GDPR)
  • Right to rectification (Art. 16 GDPR)
  • Right to erasure (Art. 17 GDPR)
  • Right to restriction of processing (Art. 18 GDPR)
  • Right to data portability (Art. 20 GDPR)
  • Right to object (Art. 21 GDPR), especially against processing based on legitimate interests
  • Right to withdraw consent at any time (Art. 7(3) GDPR), if processing is based on consent

To exercise your rights, contact us using the details in section “Controller”.

7. Supervisory authority

You have the right to lodge a complaint with a data protection supervisory authority, in particular in the EU member state of your habitual residence, place of work, or place of the alleged infringement.

8. Changes to this policy

We may update this Privacy Policy from time to time. The “Last updated” date at the top indicates the latest version.
